# CONTENT_GUIDELINES.md

# Philmont Trek 12-15 Field Guide
## Editorial & Content Standards

## Purpose

This guide should inspire curiosity while providing accurate, practical, and engaging information for Scouts hiking Trek 12-15. Every page should help readers understand the landscape, appreciate its history, and make meaningful observations in the field.

---

# Writing Principles

- Write for Scouts (ages 14+) and adult advisors.
- Explain scientific concepts in plain language.
- Be technically accurate.
- Encourage observation rather than lecture.
- Connect geology, ecology, history, and Scouting.
- Clearly distinguish facts from interpretation.
- Use active voice.

---

# Tone

- Educational
- Curious
- Respectful
- Inspiring
- Field-guide style
- Never sensationalize archaeology or Indigenous history.

---

# Daily Page Standards

## Scout Law Theme
- Introduce one point of the Scout Law.
- 75–125 words.
- Connect naturally to the day's hike.

## Clone Wars Quote
- One short quote.
- Explain why it relates to today's theme.

## Today's Mission
- 3–5 learning objectives.
- Written as observable outcomes.

## Voices from the Land
Length: 150–300 words

Include:
- Indigenous history relevant to the location.
- Archaeological evidence.
- Traditional ecological knowledge where well documented.
- Respect for cultural sites.
- Clearly separate evidence from interpretation.

## Hike Summary
Length: 150–250 words

Describe:
- Terrain
- Major landmarks
- Highlights
- Why today matters in the overall trek.

## Trek Statistics

Always include:
- Date
- Route
- Mileage
- Elevation gain/loss
- Start elevation
- End elevation
- Highest elevation
- Camp
- Estimated hiking time
- Difficulty

## Trail Conditions

Include:
- Weather expectations
- Water availability
- Altitude considerations
- Lightning awareness (when applicable)

## Sun & Moon

Always include actual values:
- Sunrise
- Sunset
- Moonrise
- Moonset
- Moon phase
- Astronomical twilight
- Best viewing window

## Route & Navigation

Include:
- Route map
- Elevation profile
- Key viewpoints
- Water sources
- Significant trail junctions

## Geology
Length: 300–600 words

Explain:
- Why the landscape exists.
- Rock types and ages.
- Geologic processes.
- Structural geology.
- What hikers should observe.

Avoid unnecessary jargon.

## History
Length: 150–300 words

Include:
- Mining
- Ranching
- Philmont history
- Local historical events

## Natural History

Provide practical field identification for:
- Trees
- Wildflowers
- Birds
- Mammals
- Reptiles
- Insects

Mention seasonal observations when appropriate.

## Things to Look For

Use checklists.
Each item should be something hikers can realistically observe.

## Photography

Recommend:
- Best viewpoints
- Time of day
- Macro opportunities
- Wildlife ethics
- Night photography

## Night Sky

Include:
- Moon phase
- Constellations
- Planets
- Milky Way
- Deep sky object
- Binocular challenge
- Campfire astronomy topic

## Fun Facts
3–5 facts.
Less than 40 words each.

## Daily Riddle
One original riddle related to geology, nature, history, or astronomy.

## Daily Challenge
Interactive activity requiring observation or teamwork.

## Scout Reflection
4–6 reflection questions tied to the Scout Law theme.

## Journal
Leave generous blank space.
Prompt:
- Favorite moment
- Best photo
- Wildlife
- Geology
- Night sky
- Lessons learned

---

# Sources

Prefer:
- USGS
- New Mexico Bureau of Geology
- Peer-reviewed papers
- Tribal resources
- U.S. Forest Service
- Philmont publications

Document all references.

---

# Image Standards

Every page should include:
- Route map
- Elevation profile
- 2–4 photographs
- 1–2 diagrams or illustrations
- Consistent icons
- High-contrast callout boxes

Never embed important text in raster artwork.

---

# Accessibility

- Clear headings
- High color contrast
- Color-independent legends
- Minimum 10 pt body text (Field Edition)
- Captions for figures

---

# Editorial Checklist

Before publishing each day:

- Scout Law complete
- Clone Wars quote included
- Voices from the Land reviewed
- Geology verified
- History verified
- Astronomy updated
- Sun/Moon times correct
- Maps updated
- Photos captioned
- Sources documented
- Spelling and grammar checked
- Journal prompts included
