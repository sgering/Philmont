# Daily Riddle

Note: Day 1 = 2026-06-28. Dates run sequentially (Day 2 = 2026-06-29, etc.).

This section provides a daily riddle for each day of the trek. Share these at camp to challenge scouts and keep spirits high.

## Day 1 — 2026-06-28
- Riddle: I have a face and hands, but no arms or legs. I tell you something important, but I don't speak. What am I?
- Answer: A watch (or clock)

## Day 2 — 2026-06-29
- Riddle: I'm always coming but never arrive. Hikers dread me in summer, but I'm essential in winter. What am I?
- Answer: Tomorrow

## Day 3 — 2026-06-30
- Riddle: The more you take, the more you leave behind. I'm measured in steps and footprints. What am I?
- Answer: Footprints (or your trail)

## Day 4 — 2026-07-01
- Riddle: I have a head and a tail but no body. I'm flipped to make decisions. What am I?
- Answer: A coin

## Day 5 — 2026-07-02
- Riddle: I speak without a mouth and hear without ears. I have no body, but I come alive with wind. What am I?
- Answer: An echo

## Day 6 — 2026-07-03
- Riddle: What has a ring but no finger, can sing but has no mouth, and is used by scouts everywhere?
- Answer: A bell (or a doorbell)

## Day 7 — 2026-07-04
- Riddle: I'm tall when young and short when old. I provide light in the dark. The wind is my enemy. What am I?
- Answer: A candle

## Day 8 — 2026-07-05
- Riddle: What gets wetter the more it dries?
- Answer: A towel

## Day 9 — 2026-07-06
- Riddle: I have cities but no houses, mountains but no trees, and water but no fish. What am I?
- Answer: A map

## Day 10 — 2026-07-07
- Riddle: I'm light as air but strong enough to hold the heaviest things. You can't see me or touch me, but without me nothing moves. What am I?
- Answer: Wind (or air)

## Day 11 — 2026-07-08
- Riddle: What is seen in the middle of March and April that can't be seen at the beginning or end of either month?
- Answer: The letter R

## Day 12 — 2026-07-09
- Riddle: I have lakes but no water, roads but no cars, and forests but no trees. Yet you can hold me in your hand. What am I?
- Answer: A map (or globe)
