# Daily What You Will See

This guide summarizes the key landmarks, camps, and landscape features for each day of the Philmont Trek 12-15 itinerary, based on trek route data and reference materials.

## Day 0 — Travel to Philmont
- Camping Headquarters arrival and opening campfire
- Orientation around base camp facilities
- Initial view of the surrounding Sangre de Cristo foothills

## Day 1 — Base Camp
- Base Camp gear checks and crew preparation
- Philmont staff camp and trailside information
- First glimpses of high-country terrain and forested ridges

## Day 2 — Camping Headquarters to Harlan
- Bus drop at Turkey Creek Trailhead
- Climb from lower canyon country into pined ridgelines
- Arrival at Harlan Staff Camp at 7,550 ft
- Ranger training area and dry camp surroundings

## Day 3 — Harlan → Devils Wash Basin
- Forested canyons and pines along the route
- Remote high-country basin scenery
- Overnight at Devil's Wash Basin Trail Camp at 8,515 ft

## Day 4 — Devils Wash Basin → Cimarron River
- Descend to the Cimarron River corridor
- High desert river valley and wet meadow contrast
- Ute Gulch food pickup pass-through
- Trail Camp views of river and sagebrush country

## Day 5 — Cimarron River → Santa Claus
- River canyon hiking with expansive valley views
- Historic and ranchland features near Santa Claus
- Dry camp in high prairie country at 8,438 ft

## Day 6 — Santa Claus → Baldy Town
- Climb into volcanic terrain and rugged ridgelines
- Approach to Baldy Town Staff Camp at 9,851 ft
- Food pickup and Baldy Summit preparation

## Day 7 — Baldy Town layover
- High-elevation Baldy Mountain day
- Mining ruins, historic Baldy Town, and summit vistas
- Broad alpine meadows and rock outcrops

## Day 8 — Baldy Town → Pueblano
- Descend from high elevation to lush creek country
- Continental Tie and lumber program area
- Overnight at Pueblano Staff Camp and forested valley views

## Day 9 — Pueblano → Elkhorn
- Ridge-top hiking and panoramic high-country views
- Elk country and conservation/trail construction scenery
- Trail Camp setting at Elkhorn with open skies

## Day 10 — Elkhorn → Ponil
- Long ridge descent into open meadows and lower country
- Arrival at Ponil Staff Camp at 7,150 ft
- Chuckwagon dinner atmosphere and ranch-style camp life

## Day 11 — Ponil → Indian Writings
- Hart Peak area and archaeology program region
- Ancient rock art and cultural landscape along the route
- Overnight at Indian Writings Staff Camp with canyon views

## Day 12 — Indian Writings → Base Camp
- Final return toward Six Mile Gate Trailhead
- Transition from backcountry to Philmont base camp lowlands
- Closing campfire and farewell at Camping Headquarters
